Brendan Eich

Father of Javascript

Here is a timeline of Brendan Eich's life:

  • 1961:
    Born on July 4th in Pittsburgh
  • 1983:
    Graduated with a Bachelor’s degree in Mathematics from the University of California, Berkeley.
  • 1985:
    Earned a Master’s degree in Computer Science from UCB.
  • 1995:
    Joined Netscape Communications Corporation, creating JavaScript in 10 days.
  • 1996:
    JavaScript was officially released with Netscape Navigator 3.0.
  • 1997:
    JavaScript became an ECMA standard (ECMAScript).
  • 1998:
    Co-founded the Mozilla project, promoting open standards.
  • 2004:
    Became the Chief Technology Officer (CTO) of Mozilla.
  • 2014:
    Appointed CEO of Mozilla Corporation, focusing on an open internet.
  • 2015:
    Co-founded Brave Software, focusing on privacy.
  • 2020s:
    Brendan Eich remains active in the tech industry.

His advocacy for open standards and user privacy reflects his commitment to empowering the tech community. Eich's legacy continues to inspire countless developers, highlighting the transformative power of innovation in shaping our digital world.
